General remarks

The output required is essentially the same as that required for the recent ASTEX intercomparion ASTEX ouput. As with the ASTEX intercomparison, we prefer to receive the files in NetCDF format. For the definitions of specific quantities, please refer to the data description of the RICO case for the LES fields and CGILS for the SCM data.


The set below gives the minimum LES data set to be submitted. Please feel free to add any other variable like mass flux statistics as requested for previous LES intercomparison cases.


General definitions of requested diagnostics

thetali = theta - Lv/(cp Pi) ql - Li/(cp Pi) qi_tot

where, ql in liquid water mixing ration, qi_tot is the total ice water mixing ratio, Lv is the the latent heat of water and Li is the latent heat of ice, Pi the exner function using p_ref = 1000 hPa

ql = liquid water specific humidity (cloud water)

qi = ice water specific humidity

qv = water vapor specific humidity

qr = rain water specific humidity

qt = total water specific humidity (ql + qr + qi + qs + qg)

LWP = liquid water path (on the basis of ql)

RWP = rain wate path based on qr

IWP = ice water path based on qi

SWP = snow water path based on qs

GWP = graupel water path based on qg


The above are general definitions and we accept that some models do not consist of all these prognostics, so if you can provide what you can and explicitly state what you are submitting, e.g. If your model only has one ice prognostic, please provide that as qi and state that qs and qg are "n/a"


How and where should you submit your data to?


We will arrange an anonymous ftp site where you can submit your output but for now once data is ready could participants email Stephan de Roode for details of the site.


Requested files fall into the four general categories,i.e. 3-D instantaneous, Initial profiles, Scalar timeseries and profile timeseries (table 1)

When submitting data, if possible, could you use the following file naming convention, institution_filekind_Lx_Dx_Nc_liquid/ice.nc, where

  • {MODEL} is a unique character string describing your model, e.g. DALES or UKMOLEM for the Met Office LES
  • {VARIABLE} is the variable in each file
  • {Lx} is a 3 letter code for horizontal domain size, e.g. 96 km = 096
  • {Dx} is a 4 character code for the horizontal resolution, e.g. 0.5 km = 0.50
  • {Nc} is a 3 letter code for the cloud drop number, e.g. 50 /cm^3 = 050
  • {liquid/ice} is a 1 letter character to describe whether the simulation is liquid only or ice e.g. L = liquid, I = ice

Instantaneous 3D data fields

To facilitate a detailed analysis of the model data, we ask the participants to save instantaneous 3D fields of the prognostic variables for the cold-air outbreak intercomparison study. In particular, air temperature T [K}, the liquid water potential temperature Tl [K], water vapour mixing ratio qv[g/kg], the total water content qt [g/kg], the cloud liquid water content qc [g/kg], the rain water content qr [g/kg], the rain droplet concentration Nr [cm^-3], the three components of the wind velocity u,v, and w [m/s], and the sub-grid TKE, e [m^2/s^2]. In this way the data can be conditionally sampled offline with arbitrary sampling criteria, e.g. cloud core, cloud, updrafts, strongest thermals etc. In addition, energy spectra can be calculated which will provide insight to the spatial structure of the vertical turbulent transport and the spatial distribution of cloud liquid water, temperature etc.


In addition, to the fields above, if participants run with ice microphysics on could they provide 3-D fields of total ice and the individual ice species, i.e. ice mass (qi [g/kg]), snow mass (qs [g/kg]), graupel mass (qg [g/kg]), ice number conc. (Ni [cm^-3]), snow number conc. (Ns [cm^-3]), graupel number (Ng [cm^-3]), We recognize that due to differences in microphysics not all fields can be populated by all participants. However, if you are providing ice fields could you also provide details of (or a reference) your microphysics schemes e.g. number of moments, particle size distribution and sedimentation parametrisations and finally ice nucleation parameterisations (and forms of ice nucleation included).


We would like these 3-D fields to be provided every 30 mins with all variables provided in one data file. Please also provide the heights, i.e. full (zf) or half (zh) and the mean pressure value at any height.


As well as the 30 minute output, could participants provide 3-D output every 5 minutes for the penultimate hour of the simulation in another file. Once again please provide the heights for the diagnostic fields.


Instantaneous profiles of the initial state

Dimensions:

  • {zf} Number of layers (full level)
  • {zh} Number of layers (half level)


Dependent variables, indexed (zf):

  • {zf} Altitude of layer mid-points (full level) [m]
  • {u} Zonal wind [m/s]
  • {v} Meridional wind [m/s]
  • {thetali} Liquid and ice water potential temperature [K]
  • {qt} Total water (vapor+liquid+ice) [g/kg]
  • {rho} density [kg/m^3]
  • {pres} pressure [Pa]

Please give the absolute values for the shortwave and longwave radiative fluxes based on the initial profiles but calculated at 12 UTC (to obtain solar radiative fluxes), indexed (zh):

  • {SW_up} Upward shortwave radiation [W/m^2]
  • {SW_dn} Downward shortwave radiation [W/m^2]
  • {LW_up} Upward longwave radiation [W/m^2]
  • {LW_dn} Downward longwave radiation [W/m^2]

Time series of scalars

Instantaneous values are to be provided at an interval of 60 s for the duration of each simulation

Dimensions:

  • {time} Number of output times

Variables:

  • {time} - Time [s]
  • {zi} - Mean height of grid cells with largest potential temperature gradient [m]
  • {zcb} - Height of bottom of lowermost cloudy grid cell [m]
  • {zct} - Height of top of highest cloudy grid cell [m]
  • {zcbmn} - Mean height of bottom of lowermost cloudy grid cells [m]
  • {zctmn} - Mean height of top of lowermost cloudy grid cells [m]
  • {LWP} - Mean liquid water path [g/m^2]
  • {LWP_var} - Liquid water path variance [g^2/m^4]
  • {RWP} - Mean rain water path [g/m^2]
  • {IWP_tot} - Mean total ice water path [g/m^2]
  • {IWP_only} - Mean ice crystal water path [g/m^2] if available
  • {SWP} - Mean snow water path [g/m^2] if available
  • {GWP} - Mean graupel water path [g/m^2] if available
  • {cc} - Fraction of columns with number of cloudy grid cells > 0 [-]
  • {we} - Entrainment rate [m/s], to be computed from the maximum thl or qt gradient in each column
  • {SST} - Sea Surface Temperature [0C]
  • {SSA} - Sea Surface Albedo [0-1]
  • {shf} - Mean upward surface sensible heat flux [K m/s]
  • {lhf} - Mean upward surface latent heat flux [kg/kg m/s]
  • {Nc} - Mean cloud droplet concentration [cm^-3]
  • {Nr} - Mean rain drop concentration [cm^-3]
  • {tke} - Vertically integrated TKE (sgs plus resolved), not density weighted [m^3/s^2]
  • {SW_alb_TOA} - Ratio downwelling/upwelling shortwave radiation at TOA [0-1]
  • {prec_srf} - Mean (downward) precipitation flux at the surface [kg/kg m/s], (or [W/m^2] or [mm/day])
  • {prec_fracsrf} - Fraction of surface grid cells with a surface precipitation flux of 3.65e-5 kg/kg m/s or higher[-]

Time series of 10 minute averaged profiles

Profiles are to be averaged over 600 s intervals for the duration of each simulation. The average over the first hour (1-600 s) is given at t = 600 s etc.
Dimensions:

  • {time} Number of output times (24)
  • {zf} Number of layers (full level)


Independent variables:

  • {time} Time [s]
  • {zf} Altitude of layer mid-points (full level) [m]


Dependent variables, indexed (time, zf):

  • {u} Zonal wind [m/s]
  • {v} Meridional wind [m/s]
  • {pres} Pressure [Pa]
  • {thetal} Liquid water potential temperature [K]
  • {qt} Total water (vapor+liquid+ice+snow+graupel) [g/kg]
  • {ql} cloud water [g/kg] (no rain)
  • {qr} Rain water[g/kg]
  • {Nr} Rain number[cm^-3]
  • {qi} ice water[g/kg]
  • {Ni} ice number[cm^-3]
  • {qs} snow water[g/kg]
  • {Ns} snow number[cm^-3]
  • {qg} graupel water[g/kg]
  • {Ng} graupel number[cm^-3]
  • {u_var} Resolved variance of zonal wind [m^2/s^2]
  • {v_var} Resolved variance of meridional wind [m^2/s^2]
  • {w_var} Resolved variance of vertical wind [m^2/s^2]
  • {w_skw} Resolved (w'3)/(w'2)3/2 [-]
  • {thetal_var} Resolved variance of l [K^2]
  • {qt_var} Resolved variance of qt [kg^2/kg^2]
  • {tot_wthl} Total l flux, including subgrid-scale [K m/s]
  • {sgs_wthl} Subgrid-scale l flux [K m/s]
  • {tot_wqt} Total qt flux, including subgrid-scale[kg/kg m/s]
  • {sgs_wqt} Subgrid-scale qt flux [kg/kg m/s]
  • {tot_wthv} Total v flux [K m/s] , v= (1 + I qvap - qliq), with I = Rv/Rd-1 = 0.608, and Rd=287.04 J kg-1 K-1, Rv = 461.5 J kg-1 K-1, the gas constants for dry air and water vapor, respectively.
  • {tot_uw} Total (sgs plus resolved) zonal momentum flux [m^2/s^2]
  • {tot_vw} Total (sgs plus resolved) meridional momentum flux [m^2/s^2]
  • {res_tke} Resolved TKE [m^2/s^2]
  • {sgs_tke} Subgrid TKE [m^2/s^2]
  • {res_buoy} Resolved buoyancy TKE production [m^2/s^3]
  • {res_shr} Resolved shear TKE production [m^2/s^3]
  • {res_tke_transport} Resolved TKE transport [m^2/s^3]
  • {res_pres_transport} Resolved pressure transport [m^2/s^3]
  • {res_diss} (absolute value of the) TKE dissipation (explicit plus numerical) [m^2/s^3]
  • {cfrac} Fraction of cloudy grid cells [-]
  • {prec} Precipitation flux (positive downward) [kg/kg m/s]
  • {prec_prc} Precipitation flux (positive downward), averaged over precipitating grid cells only [kg/kg m/s]
  • {frac_prc} Fraction of precipitating grid cells with a precipitation flux of 3.65e-5 kg/kg m/s or higher[-]
  • {Nr} Mean rain drop concentration (see notes RICO intercomparison case) [cm^-3]
  • {Nc} Mean cloud droplet concentration (see notes RICO intercomparison case) [cm^-3]
  • Please give the absolute values for the shortwave and longwave radiative fluxes:
  • {SW_up} Upward shortwave radiation [W/m^2]
  • {SW_dn} Downward shortwave radiation [W/m^2]
  • {LW_up} Upward longwave radiation [W/m^2]
  • {LW_dn} Downward longwave radiation [W/m^2]
