Atmospheric Dynamics and Boundary Layer Meteorology

Meso-scale circulations are responsible for weather events such as sea breeze, thunderstorms, lee-waves, severe downslope windstorms, polar lows and tropical cyclones.
The research topics at IMAU in the area of mesoscale meteorology are:

  • Thermally forced circulations and waves in the coastal zone.
  • The dynamics of jet streaks and troughs in relation to the formation of severe thunderstorms over western Europe.

The atmospheric boundary layer affects both the dynamics and thermodynamics of the atmosphere. From a climate and local climate perspective the most important boundary layer processes that need to be parameterized in numerical models of the atmosphere are vertical mixing and the formation, maintenance and dissipation of clouds (stratocumulus, cumulus and fog).
The current research topics at IMAU are:

  • Analysis of aircraft observations of stratocumulus and cumulus observed during the Atlantic Stratocumulus Transition EXperiment (ASTEX).
    - ASTEX Marine Stratocumulus Case
    - EUCREM: Stratocumulus case RF06 of ASTEX
  • Investigation of the physical processes responsible for the transition from stratocumulus to shallow cumulus.
  • LES and 1D modelling of stratocumulus, cumulus and fog.
  • Entrainment processes at cloud top and the possible relationship with cloud breakup processes.
  • Diurnal decoupling of stratocumulus and clearing due to solar absorption.
  • Radiation in broken cloud fields.
