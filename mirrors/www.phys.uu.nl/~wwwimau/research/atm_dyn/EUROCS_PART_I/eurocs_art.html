FIRE I observations for the model inter-comparison of EUROCS:

Stratocumulus over the ocean

The primary objective of EUROCS is to improve the treatment of cloud systems in global and regional climate models. The present research focuses on the representation of stratocumulus over the ocean. We will consider the full diurnal cycle of stratocumulus off the coast of California based on observations of the First ISCCP (International Satellite Cloud Climatology Project) Regional Experiment (FIRE).
In Duynkerke and Teixeira (2001) the ECMWF Re-Analysis (ERA) results for July 1987 are compared with stratocumulus observations for the same period made during the FIRE I experiment. They concluded that the ERA cloud thickness is overestimated and cloud cover and liquid water path are strongly underestimated compared with the FIRE I observations. As a result the ERA downwelling shortwave radiation at the surface is much larger than observed. They argued that the large difference between the ERA downwelling shortwave radiation at the surface and that observed in FIRE I leads to large biases in the sea-surface temperature in a coupled ocean-atmosphere model.

We focus on the representation of diurnal variation of cloud properties (boundary layer depth, liquid water path, optical depth, etc.), surface energy balance and the implications for a coupled atmosphere-ocean model, turbulence dynamics (entrainment, decoupling, etc.) in the boundary layer, and shortwave optical properties of inhomogeneous 3-dimensional cloud fields. The study consists of two different parts:

Part I is intended for Large-Eddy Simulations (LES) models and Single Column Models (SCM) and
part II is for Global Circulation Models (CGM) and Regional Circulation Models (RCM).

Paper describing the setup are downloadable as compressed PostScript file. The files are eurocs_init.ps.Z and CGMeurocs_init.ps.Z for part I and part II, respectively.
