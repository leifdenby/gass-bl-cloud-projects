Set G compares the (co-)spectral energy at the 24 and 37 LT of vertical velocity (w in m/s) and total water
content (qt in kg/kg) at three heights in the boundary layer:
200 m, 500 m and at height (zm) where the buoyancy flux has its minimum at the inversion.
This are one-dimensional (co-)spectra in the x-direction and are averaged over the y-direction.
Note that the sum of the (co-)spectral energies should equal the one-dimensional (co-)variances.
