(2)Numerical Domain:

Dimension:

	above.

(3)Numerical Technique:

Numerical method: finite difference
Advection scheme: upwind scheme
Dynamical equations: -
Lateral Boundary Conditions: -
Upper Boundary Conditions: no flux
translation velocity: -

(4)Turbulence Closure Scheme:

Turbulence Closure: ensemble mean
Turbulence Closure Type: 1st order 
	K-diffusion with Richardson number. The K coefficients are linked
	to Ri with a Louis-type formulation. Two Ri and two K coefficients are
	computed: one in the clear region, the other in the cloudy region.
	K is then taken as K = f*K_cloudy + (1-f)*K_clear
	where f is the cloud fraction
	where K_cloudy relates to Ri_cloudy
	where K_clear relates to Ri_clear.
	For both cases,
	Ri = g/theta0 * buoyancy/(shear^2)
	buoyancy_clear = vertical gradient of thetav
	buoyancy_cloudy=(vert. grad. of thetav)-(theta moist adiabat)
	with shear = vert. grad. of u and v
Variables predicted by the turbulence closure:
	Eddy diffusitivies (cf. above)
Closure for dissipation rate: implicitly represented in the 
	Louis-type formulation
Closure for turbulent diffusion length scale:
	mix. length = 35. * MAX(0, (h2-z)/(h2-h1) )
	where h2 is the highest level altitude of the model
	where h1 is the lowest level altitude of the model
	where z is the local altitude
Subgrid Condensation scheme:
	Subgrid Distribution assumed, equi-repartition of water
	around the mean value, variance imposed 
	(Le Treut & Li cloud scheme)
