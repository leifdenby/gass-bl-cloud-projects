Scientist : Herve Grenier, CNRM
Model : CNRM Climate Model in 1D mode
Modifications to the standart climate model : minimal but
     1. vertical resolution increased
     2. Time step increased to 2 minutes (instead of 30' in climate 3D mode)
     3. Model top : 4200 m.
     4. SW radiation scheme different from the climate model scheme
          (for practical purposes in order to avoid the need to use a full tropsopheric-
            stratospheric column model)
     5. LW radiation prescribed as required

 Model Description :
 . Ricard and Royer (1993) coupled statistical cloud - turbulence scheme :
    assumes a production-dissipation balance for TKE and uses level
    2.5 Mellor Yamada model (modified by Galperin et al 1988) to compute
    TKE.
  Second order moments are computed after Mellor and Yamada (1974)
 . Non prognostic liquid water
 . Eulerian vertical advection scheme (Simmons and Burridge, 1981)
 . SW radiation scheme of Geleyn (1989)
 . Convection scheme of Bougeault (1985)


 Some refs :
 Ricard and Royer (1993) , Ann. Geophys., 1095-1115.
 Simmons and Burridge (1981), Month. Wea Rev, 758-766
 Bougeault (1985), Month. Wea. Rev., 2108-2121


Hint on model behaviour :
 you will see that there are some inconsistencies in the TKE budget
 (non zero residus and obviously pathologic terms near the surface)
 These problems are corrected in a new version of the scheme.

 I believe there may be an underestimation of the downward SW radiative
 flux at noon.   If it is really pathologic, I may be able to use another scheme.

Datasets:

  • setA Initial conditions
  • setB Relevant parameters for entrainment
  • setC Hourly averaged (0-1 LT, 1-2 LT, etc) values of turbulent fluxes (resolved plus subgrid), radiative fluxes and cloud properties
  • setD23.5 Horizontal mean profiles averaged over the "midnight" period of the simulation
  • setD36.5 Horizontal mean profiles averaged over the "noon" period of the simulation
  • setE23.5 Turbulent velocity variances, skewness and fluxes (23-24) average
  • setE36.5 Turbulent velocity variances, skewness and fluxes (36-37) average
  • setF23.5 Resolved turbulent kinetic energy and its budget terms (23-24) average
  • setF36.5 Resolved turbulent kinetic energy and its budget terms (36-37) average
