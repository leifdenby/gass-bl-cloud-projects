Sets B uses instantaneous values at hour (1,2,3 LT etc), and relevant parameters for entrainment closure.
  1. simulation time (hour)
  2. mean inversion height zi (in meters), defined as the horizontal average over all columns of the height
    at which the total (liquid + vapour) water content equals 8.1 g/kg.
    Determined by linear interpolation between adjacent grid levels of total water content.
  3. mean column-integrated liquid water path (in g/m^2).
  4. entrainment rate
    we = 0.5*(zi(hour)+zi(hour-1))*1.5*10^(-5) + (zi(hour)-zi(hour-1))/3600 (in m/s)
  5. wstar= (2.5*vertical integral from surface to domain top of the (hour) to (hour-1) average of <w'b'>)^(1/3) (in m/s).
    Here buoyancy flux <w'b'> = g*<w'*thetav'>theta0 (in m^2/s^3) and
    theta0 = 291.5K is the reference potential temperature
  6. deltab = g*( thetav(zi(hour-0.5) + 50m) - thetav(zi(hour-0.5) - 50m) )/ theta0 (in m/s^2)
  7. A = we* deltab*zi(hour-0.5)/wstar^3
